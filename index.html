FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about converting files to GIF format.

How do I create an animated GIF from images?

Select the "Images to GIF" tab, then upload two or more images (PNG, JPG, WebP, BMP). Arrange them in the desired order by dragging thumbnails, set your frame speed and output size, then click "Convert to GIF." Each image becomes one frame of the animation.

How many images can I combine into one GIF?

There is no hard limit. You can add as many images as you like. Keep in mind that more frames and larger dimensions will produce bigger files and take longer to process. For best results, we recommend keeping it under 100 frames.

Can I reorder the images after uploading?

Yes. Simply drag and drop the thumbnails to rearrange the frame order. You can also remove individual frames by hovering over a thumbnail and clicking the delete button, or add more images at any time.

Are my files uploaded to a server?

No. All file processing happens 100% locally in your browser using JavaScript. Your images and videos never leave your device. We have zero access to your files, making this one of the most private GIF converters available online.

How do I control GIF speed and file size?

Use the frame delay slider to control animation speed (lower = faster). The quality slider affects file size: 1 = best quality (larger file), 20 = faster encoding (smaller file). You can also reduce the output dimensions to shrink the final GIF.

Can I convert a specific portion of a video?

Yes. In the "Video to GIF" tab, upload a video and set a custom start time, duration, and playback speed. You can also adjust the frame rate from 1 to 30 FPS to control smoothness and file size.

What is the maximum GIF resolution supported?

You can set the output resolution up to 2000x2000 pixels. For most use cases, 480-640px wide GIFs offer a good balance between quality and file size.

Is this tool really free?

Absolutely. Squoosh GIF Converter is 100% free with no hidden costs, no sign-up required, and no watermarks on your output files. We believe essential conversion tools should be accessible to everyone.
